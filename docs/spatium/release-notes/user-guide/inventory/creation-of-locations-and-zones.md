# Creation of Locations and Zones

To begin configuring parking locations, you can manage your different locations and their zones from the inventory area. Start by creating a site: click the “Create a Location” button in the top right of the screen to set up its name and address, so it can appear on the map. When you click this icon, the floating menu “Create a New Location” will open.

**Note**: Only administrators are allowed to create and modify locations. If you are unable to see the “Create a Zone” button, contact your administrator to review your permissions.



To create a zone, first enter the location that will become the parent of the new zone by selecting it. Then, click the “Create Zone” button in the top right of the screen, and the floating menu “New Zone” will open.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (36).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (37).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can then configure the zone by assigning it a name, specifying its type, and, for a “2D Floor Plan” type zone, uploading its 2D plan in JPEG or PNG format by dragging the plan into the designated box.

Newly created locations and zones automatically appear in the inventory after they are created. Depending on your permissions, you can also edit or delete locations and zones by clicking on the “Edit Location” or “Edit Zone” button at the top right of their respective pages. To delete a location or zone, simply hover over the arrow to the right of the “Edit Location” or “Edit Zone” icon, and the “Delete” button will appear.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (38).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (39).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

By clicking on “Edit Zone”, the “Edit Zone” dialog will open. This window allows you to change the zone's name, select the zone type (map zone, 2D floor plan, or API camera image), add a plan image, modify display settings, and access advanced data source settings.

For advanced data source settings, click on the icon to open a more detailed menu. You can choose between Spatium Sensors (automatic data from occupancy sensors) and Parknet Counter (data from a partner) to determine the data source for this zone.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (40).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

For display settings, you can choose between Parknet or System TV, which represent two types of screens that can be used in the zone.
