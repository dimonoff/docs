# Configuration of Dynamic Display Panels

Spatium allows you to integrate your dynamic display panels to show the number of available spots and visualize their status within the application. It is also possible to deactivate a panel if you need to indicate to your users that a section of your parking is no longer available for use due to construction or other special events.

To add a new panel, enter edit mode by clicking the “Spatium Toolbox” button to configure and modify dynamic display panels in the relevant zone.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (57).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

You can add new dynamic display panels by typing “Add a Smart Panel”. A floating menu will appear on the left side of the screen.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (58).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (59).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



The panel will then appear in the center of the layout, already selected, and a floating menu will appear on the left side of the screen to configure the following:

* Name
* Assigned spots – The set of spots for which you want to display availability by selecting the groups and zones that the panel will consider
* Data Source Settings



**Notes:**

* Any spot that is part of two or more sets (zones or groups) assigned to the same panel will be counted as unique. Spatium automatically recognizes and eliminates duplicates from the counters.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (60).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Once configured, click on “Apply” in the floating menu, and then “Save” at the bottom of the screen.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (61).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

By entering edit mode and selecting a display panel, an edit dialog will open on the left side of the screen. This dialog allows you to modify the display settings and access advanced data source settings.

For advanced data source settings, click on the icon to open a more detailed menu. You can choose between Spatium Sensors (automatic data from occupancy sensors) and Parknet Counter (data from a partner) to determine the data source for this panel.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (62).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

For display settings, click on the “Display Settings” tab and choose between Parknet, System TV or Signal-Tech, which represent the panel manufacturer of your smart panel.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (63).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Once the manufacturer/partner is selected, you can set its identifier so Spatium can communicate with it and display live availability.



* The unique identifier will be provided by the manufacturer
